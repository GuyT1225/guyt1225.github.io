    Why PDF Copy-Paste Text Is So Messy

    PDFs are a display format, not a text format. When a PDF is rendered, text is laid out at fixed positions on the page. When you copy from a PDF reader, the software attempts to reconstruct the original text flow, but it frequently inserts extra line breaks at the end of every printed line, soft hyphens (U+00AD) that were used for hyphenation, non-breaking spaces where the typesetter needed fixed spacing, and sometimes ligature characters like ﬁ (fi) and ﬂ (fl) that don't match ordinary letter sequences.

    The result is text that looks correct when read but fails in databases, search indexes, CMS systems, and anywhere that processes text character-by-character. PasteLint's Deep Clean removes all of this invisibly.
